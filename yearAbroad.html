My Year Abroad at VU Amsterdam

In my third year of BSc Computer Science at the University of Leicester, I spent an academic year abroad at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (VU Amsterdam) in the Netherlands. This exchange has been an incredible opportunity to explore new learning environments, collaborate internationally, and apply my technical and creative skills in unique ways.

A key highlight of this course was my participation in the Robot Interaction Hackathon, where I collaborated with teammates to develop an interactive scenario using the NAO humanoid robot.

This project allowed me to merge my interests in AI-driven interaction design and human-computer communication, while tackling real-world applications in healthcare and assistive technology. It also helped refine my teamwork and problem-solving skills as we worked within time constraints to deliver a natural and engaging robot behavior.

This course focuses on formalisms for representing knowledge and data, particularly for reuse and publication on the web. Key topics include the Semantic Web stack (RDF, RDFS, OWL), the SPARQL query language, and building data science pipelines that integrate data and knowledge for analysis and visualization.

For the milestone assignment, I worked on a group project where I designed an ontology to structure and represent the knowledge. The project required creating an initial draft of the final report including preliminary results for the first version of the data analysis pipeline, two datasets (one RDF, one non-RDF), and a conceptual ontology design.

The analysis was aimed at stakeholders who needed structured, queryable knowledge to support decision-making. Competency questions were formulated to ensure the knowledge graph and visualization pipeline met their needs.

I also identified an external ontology to reuse, along with RDF and non-RDF datasets to populate the graph, and defined a preliminary methodology, domain, and scope.
